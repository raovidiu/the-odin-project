Cake


Description

Here’s a straightforward chocolate cake recipe perfect for beginners and seasoned bakers alike. Combine 1¾ cups all-purpose flour, ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder, 2 cups granulated sugar, 1½ teaspoons baking powder, 1½ teaspoons baking soda, and 1 teaspoon salt in a large bowl, then whisk in 2 large eggs, 1 cup whole milk, ½ cup vegetable oil, and 2 teaspoons vanilla extract until smooth. Add 1 cup boiling water to create a thin batter, pour it into two greased 9-inch round pans, and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30-35 minutes—done when a toothpick comes out clean. After cooling, frost with your choice of chocolate or vanilla frosting for a rich, moist cake that’s simple yet satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour, 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder, 2 cups granulated sugar, 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder, 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda, and 1 teaspoon salt.
  3. Add 2 large eggs, 1 cup whole milk, 1/2 cup vegetable oil, and 2 teaspoons vanilla extract to the dry ingredients, mixing until fully combined and smooth.
  4. Stir in 1 cup boiling water to thin the batter—it will be runny, but that’s normal.
  5. Pour the batter evenly into the prepared cake pans.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, frost with your favorite chocolate or vanilla frosting and serve.
