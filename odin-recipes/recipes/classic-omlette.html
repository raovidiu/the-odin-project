The Perfect Classic French Omlette (no idea what AI halucinated in the image above)

A classic French omelette is smooth, curd-free, and almond-shaped. Unlike the diner-style "country" omelette, the French version is cooked quickly over high heat and rolled into a cylinder. The result is a creamy, slightly undercooked interior (baveuse) that melts in your mouth. It is the ultimate test of patience and technique.

Ingredients

  • 3 large farm-fresh eggs (at room temperature)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped chives (optional)
  • A pinch of white pepper (to keep the color uniform)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, crack the eggs and whisk vigorously with a fork until no streaks of white remain. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add the butter. Let it foam, but do not let it brown.
  3. Pour the eggs into the pan and stir constantly with a heat-resistant spatula, shaking the pan at the same time as if making scrambled eggs.
  4. When the eggs are mostly set but still slightly moist on top, use the spatula to smooth them into an even layer.
  5. Tilt the pan and carefully roll the omelette onto itself, starting from the handle side. Slide it onto a plate, seam-side down.
